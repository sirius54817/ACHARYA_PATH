National Education Policy 2020 - Overview

A comprehensive framework to guide the development of education in India

The National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020) was approved by the Union Cabinet of India on 29 July 2020 and outlines the vision of India's new education system. The new policy replaces the previous National Policy on Education, 1986 and aims to transform India's education system by 2040.

Universal Access

Ensuring inclusive and equitable quality education for all

Holistic Development

Focus on cognitive, social, emotional and physical development

Global Standards

Education aligned with 21st century needs and SDG4

School Education
New Pedagogical Structure (5+3+3+4)

The policy introduces a new pedagogical structure replacing the existing 10+2 structure with a 5+3+3+4 structure corresponding to ages 3-8, 8-11, 11-14, and 14-18 years. This includes:

  • Foundational Stage (3-8 years): 3 years of pre-school and grades 1-2
  • Preparatory Stage (8-11 years): Grades 3-5
  • Middle Stage (11-14 years): Grades 6-8
  • Secondary Stage (14-18 years): Grades 9-12

School Education
Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE)

The policy emphasizes the critical importance of early years (age 3-6) by including it in the formal education structure. Key points include:

  • Universal access to ECCE to ensure all students are school-ready
  • ECCE will be delivered through a significantly expanded and strengthened system of institutions
  • Prior to the age of 5, ECCE will consist of flexible, multi-level, play-based, activity-based, and inquiry-based learning
  • A National Curricular and Pedagogical Framework for Early Childhood Care and Education (NCPFECCE) will be developed by NCERT

Higher Education
Higher Education Restructuring

The policy proposes significant restructuring of higher education institutions with a vision to create vibrant multidisciplinary institutions. Key reforms include:

  • Moving towards multidisciplinary universities and colleges with 3,000+ students
  • Phasing out single-stream institutions over time
  • All universities and colleges to aim to become multidisciplinary by 2040
  • Multiple entry and exit options with appropriate certification
  • Undergraduate degrees can be of 3 or 4 years duration
  • Academic Bank of Credit for storing academic credits earned

Higher Education
Regulatory Framework Reforms

The policy proposes significant changes to the regulatory system of higher education:

  • Establishment of a single regulator for higher education - the Higher Education Commission of India (HECI)
  • The HECI will have four independent verticals:
    • National Higher Education Regulatory Council (NHERC)
    • National Accreditation Council (NAC)
    • Higher Education Grants Council (HEGC)
    • General Education Council (GEC)
  • Common entrance exams for university admissions to be conducted by the National Testing Agency

Teacher Education
Teacher Education Reforms

The policy recognizes teachers as the heart of the learning process and proposes major reforms in teacher preparation:

  • By 2030, the minimum degree qualification for teaching will be a 4-year integrated B.Ed. degree
  • All teacher education programs will be moved into multidisciplinary universities and colleges
  • A new National Curriculum Framework for Teacher Education (NCFTE) will be formulated by the NCTE
  • A National Mission for Mentoring will be established
  • A robust merit-based structure of tenure, promotion, and salary structure will be developed

Vocational Education
Vocational Education Integration

The policy aims to integrate vocational education into mainstream education:

  • Vocational education will be integrated into all school and higher education institutions in a phased manner
  • By 2025, at least 50% of learners shall have exposure to vocational education
  • A 10-day bagless period during grades 6-8 to learn vocational crafts from local artisans
  • NCIVE, established under NCERT, will develop National Skills Qualifications Framework
  • Higher education institutions will offer vocational courses integrated with the general education curriculum

School Education
Medium of Instruction and Language Policy

The policy emphasizes mother tongue/local language as the medium of instruction at least until grade 5, preferably till grade 8 and beyond:

  • Focus on mother tongue/local language/regional language as medium of instruction
  • Three-language formula to continue with greater flexibility
  • No language will be imposed on any state
  • Sanskrit to be offered at all levels of school and higher education
  • Other classical languages also to be available as options
  • Foreign languages offered at the secondary level

Higher Education
Research and Innovation

The policy emphasizes research and innovation across disciplines to make India a knowledge superpower:

  • Establishment of a National Research Foundation (NRF) to fund outstanding research
  • Focus on promoting research in universities and colleges
  • NRF will coordinate with multiple funding agencies to ensure synergy of operations
  • Research focus on both basic and applied research as well as translational research
  • Special research funds for startups and technology development
